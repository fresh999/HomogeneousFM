Flow matching on homogeneous spaces.

Let the homogeneous space be G/H

Warning: we need to be given representatives of equivalence classes as data points. Alternatively, if the homogeneous space is "representable", we need a global section of G -> G/H.

We do FM on G. We interpolate to create a conditional probability path and we get the corresponding vector field.

--> in case the distributions (both the dataset and the empirical one) are not H-invariant, we need to augment them;
--> the model needs to be H-equivariant (which in mathematically correct terms means the following: the vector field we are learning is invariant under the pushforward by right-multiplications R_h for all h in H).
